Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about UAE corporate banking and our services

What is the minimum balance requirement for UAE corporate accounts?

Minimum balance requirements vary by bank and account type, ranging from AED 10,000 for basic business accounts to AED 100,000 for premium services. We help you find banks that match your cash flow needs and negotiate better terms based on your business profile.

How long does it take to open a corporate bank account in UAE?

Account opening typically takes 2-6 weeks depending on the bank, business type, and completeness of documentation. UAE national banks (Emirates NBD, ADCB) are generally faster (2-4 weeks), while international banks may take 4-6 weeks due to additional compliance requirements.

What documents are required for account opening?

Standard requirements include trade license, memorandum of association, board resolution, shareholders' passports, and Emirates ID for UAE residents. Free zone and offshore companies have slightly different requirements. We provide a complete checklist and help with document preparation.

Can I open accounts with multiple banks?

Yes, many businesses maintain accounts with multiple banks for diversification, better service access, and competitive rates. We recommend a primary relationship bank plus 1-2 secondary banks. This strategy provides backup options and access to different banking products and services.

Are Islamic banking options available?

Yes, UAE offers comprehensive Islamic banking services through institutions like ADIB, Dubai Islamic Bank, and Mashreq Al Islami. These provide Sharia-compliant products including Murabaha financing, Ijara leasing, and Islamic investment accounts. All major conventional banks also offer Islamic windows.

Can offshore companies open UAE bank accounts?

Yes, but with additional requirements and longer processing times (5-10 weeks). Offshore companies need economic substance documentation, beneficial ownership declarations, and apostilled documents. We specialize in offshore banking and have strong relationships with banks that accept these structures.
